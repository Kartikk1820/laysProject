[About Nestlé]

In 1867, Henri Nestlé, a pharmacist, started a small company in Vevey, Switzerland, developing a breakthrough infant food product to help nourish babies who couldn’t be breastfed. Just a few years later, his business grew significantly, and the Nestlé name became synonymous with nutrition and health.

In 1905, Nestlé merged with the Anglo-Swiss Condensed Milk Company to form what we now know as Nestlé S.A., expanding its product offerings and cementing its role as a global leader in nutrition, health, and wellness.

Nestlé products entered Canada in 1918, bringing a wide array of nutritious food items that quickly gained popularity for their quality. By the mid-20th century, Nestlé became a household name not only in Canada but across the world. Today, Nestlé offers a vast range of products, including baby foods, bottled water, dairy products, and confectionery.

Nestlé has grown its portfolio to include numerous renowned brands, each offering consumers the same dedication to quality and innovation that Henri Nestlé stood for over 150 years ago.
